TEMPO: Prompt-based Generative Pre-trained Transformer for Time Series Forecasting

TEMPO is an innovative framework that leverages prompt-based generative pre-trained transformers for time series forecasting. Designed to overcome the limitations of traditional time series models, TEMPO excels at capturing complex temporal patterns across diverse domains, including healthcare, finance, and energy. By incorporating specialized prompts and a decomposition-based approach, TEMPO efficiently adapts to the trends, seasonality, and residual components of time series data, leading to more accurate and robust predictions.

TEMPO's architecture combines an encoder-decoder transformer structure with the power of semi-soft prompts, guiding the model to learn the underlying characteristics of time series data. This enables the effective use of zero-shot and few-shot forecasting, making it suitable for scenarios where labeled data is limited or for tasks involving unseen datasets. Moreover, TEMPO seamlessly integrates multimodal data, using contextual information such as textual inputs to enhance forecast accuracy in complex scenarios.

With its emphasis on interpretability, TEMPO not only provides accurate predictions but also helps uncover key drivers behind temporal changes. This makes it an ideal solution for industries requiring data-driven insights, anomaly detection, and strategic decision-making. TEMPO sets a new benchmark for leveraging large language models in time series analysis, bridging the gap between foundational AI technologies and practical, real-world applications.

About TEMPO

TEMPO is an advanced prompt-based generative pre-trained transformer specifically designed for time series forecasting. It excels in predicting across a variety of domains such as healthcare, transportation, finance, and beyond, all with just a few lines of code. TEMPO is ready to use as a foundational model for time series, available via public APIs or on your own infrastructure. It supports both zero-shot prediction and multi-modal input capabilities, making it a versatile choice for capturing the intricate dynamics of time series data. Get started today to experience the power of TEMPO for diverse temporal phenomena modeling.

Features and Capabilities

  • Zero-shot Inference: TEMPO can generate accurate time series forecasts without any prior training on specific datasets. This enables immediate deployment for both known and novel datasets, delivering insights directly with no additional fine-tuning required. TEMPO performs well in scenarios involving unseen data, showcasing its strong generalization abilities.
  • Prompt-based Fine-tuning: Enhance TEMPO's effectiveness through prompt-based fine-tuning. By utilizing decomposition techniques that split time series data into trend, seasonal, and residual components, and by employing specialized prompts, TEMPO adapts to unique time series characteristics. This approach allows TEMPO to refine its forecasting skills, ultimately improving prediction performance on domain-specific tasks.
  • Multi-modal Integration: TEMPO is not limited to time series alone; it also integrates multimodal data for richer, more informative predictions. It can use textual information alongside time series data, improving forecast accuracy in complex scenarios that require contextual information, such as news impact on financial time series.
  • Seamless API Access: Easily integrate TEMPO with your applications via robust APIs. Whether deploying on the cloud or on-premises, TEMPO’s APIs offer the flexibility to adapt and maintain complete control over your time series data and workflows.
  • Component Analysis for Interpretability: TEMPO provides interpretability by leveraging the inherent properties of time series data—decomposing inputs into trend, seasonality, and residuals. This decomposition enables deeper insights into the factors driving predictions and allows for clearer understanding and analysis of model results.

Architecture

  • TEMPO Architecture: TEMPO is built on an encoder-decoder structure with a series of layers, each equipped with residual connections and normalization layers. At its core, TEMPO utilizes the attention mechanism, inspired by the "Attention is All You Need" architecture, which allows it to effectively learn temporal dependencies by focusing on significant events and identifying meaningful patterns across the entire time series data. The TEMPO architecture uses time series decomposition as a key inductive bias to represent the input sequence. By breaking down the input into trend, seasonal, and residual components through local smoothing techniques, each temporal aspect is represented by unique embeddings and prompts. These decomposed components are then used to provide the model with a structured understanding of temporal behavior, improving the transformer’s ability to predict complex and diverse time series patterns.

  • Semi-soft Prompt Integration: TEMPO incorporates semi-soft prompts specifically tailored to enhance time series adaptability. By dynamically modifying prompts that represent each component—trend, seasonality, and residual—TEMPO can efficiently guide the transformer in learning from temporal information.

conda create -n tempo python=3.8

pip install -r requirements.txt

Script Demo

A streamlining example showing how to perform forecasting using TEMPO:

# Third-party library imports
import numpy as np
import torch
from numpy.random import choice

# Local imports
from models.TEMPO import TEMPO

# Load the pre-trained TEMPO model
model = TEMPO.load_pretrained_model(
    device=torch.device('cuda:0' if torch.cuda.is_available() else 'cpu'),
    repo_id="Melady/TEMPO",
    filename="TEMPO-80M_v1.pth",
    cache_dir="./checkpoints/TEMPO_checkpoints"
)

# Generate random input data
input_data = np.random.rand(336)

# Perform prediction without gradient tracking
with torch.no_grad():
    predicted_values = model.predict(input_data, pred_length=96)

print("Predicted values:")
print(predicted_values)

TimeDiT: General-Purpose Diffusion Transformers for Time Series Foundation Modeling

TimeDiT is a pioneering foundation model for time series analysis that employs a diffusion-based approach combined with Transformer architecture. Unlike traditional autoregressive models, TimeDiT generates high-quality time series data through a denoising diffusion paradigm, which effectively addresses the challenges presented by missing values, multi-resolution data, and complex temporal dependencies.

TimeDiT's architecture allows for seamless handling of various downstream tasks—including forecasting, imputation, anomaly detection, and synthetic data generation—by incorporating novel masking strategies and a channel alignment mechanism. Moreover, TimeDiT supports the integration of external domain knowledge, such as physical laws, during its sampling process without requiring additional fine-tuning. This feature ensures that generated time series adhere to known physical principles, making TimeDiT a versatile solution for diverse real-world applications.

Evaluated on over 20 datasets and benchmarked against more than 25 models, TimeDiT has demonstrated state-of-the-art or highly competitive performance across numerous challenging tasks, solidifying its position as a powerful, scalable, and adaptable foundation model for time series analysis.

TS-Reasoner: A Compositional Reasoning Framework for Complex Time Series Analysis

TS-Reasoner is a groundbreaking framework designed to go beyond traditional forecasting by enabling complex, compositional time series reasoning. Unlike existing time series models that focus solely on single-step tasks like forecasting or anomaly detection, TS-Reasoner excels in handling intricate multi-step reasoning tasks that integrate domain knowledge and user-defined constraints. By leveraging large language models (LLMs) to decompose complex questions into structured programs, TS-Reasoner seamlessly synthesizes time series data and domain expertise to provide end-to-end solutions.

The model combines advanced time series analysis with LLM-aided reasoning, allowing for the creation of custom modules and flexible task decomposition. This enables TS-Reasoner to perform diverse tasks, such as decision-making, compositional question answering, and causal analysis—all while optimizing intermediate steps cohesively. Our comprehensive experiments demonstrate that TS-Reasoner outperforms state-of-the-art methods, highlighting its potential in solving real-world, multi-step time series challenges.
